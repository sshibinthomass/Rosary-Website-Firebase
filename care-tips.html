Plant Care Tips

Proper plant care is essential for keeping your green friends happy and healthy. Here are some helpful tips and guides to help your plants thrive!

Watering Tips

  • Water most plants when the top inch of soil feels dry
  • Use room temperature water to avoid shocking the roots
  • Water thoroughly until it drains from the bottom
  • Reduce watering in winter when plant growth slows down
  • Succulents and cacti need less frequent watering
  • Tropical plants often prefer consistently moist soil

Light Requirements

  • Most houseplants need bright, indirect light
  • South-facing windows provide the most light
  • East and west windows offer moderate light
  • North windows provide low light
  • Rotate plants regularly for even growth
  • Use sheer curtains to filter harsh direct sunlight

Temperature & Humidity

  • Most houseplants thrive in temperatures between 18-24°C
  • Avoid placing plants near drafts, heaters, or AC vents
  • Tropical plants prefer higher humidity (50-60%)
  • Use a humidifier or pebble tray to increase humidity
  • Group plants together to create a microclimate
  • Misting can help but only provides temporary humidity

Soil & Fertilizing

  • Use well-draining potting mix appropriate for your plant type
  • Fertilize during the growing season (spring and summer)
  • Use half-strength fertilizer to avoid burning roots
  • Don't fertilize newly repotted plants or dormant plants
  • Succulents and cacti need special cactus soil
  • Repot when roots start growing through drainage holes

Pruning & Maintenance

  • Remove yellow or brown leaves regularly
  • Prune leggy growth to encourage bushier plants
  • Clean leaves with a damp cloth to remove dust
  • Use clean, sharp scissors for pruning
  • Spring is the best time for major pruning
  • Pinch back growth tips to encourage branching

Pest Control

  • Inspect new plants before bringing them home
  • Isolate infected plants immediately
  • Treat with neem oil or insecticidal soap
  • Wipe leaves regularly to prevent pest infestations
  • Maintain good air circulation around plants
  • Avoid overwatering which can lead to fungus gnats

Seasonal Care Guide

Spring

Increase watering as growth resumes, repot if needed, start fertilizing, and clean dusty leaves.

Summer

Provide extra humidity, protect from intense sun, water more frequently, and watch for pests.

Fall

Reduce watering and fertilizing, clean windows for maximum light, and prepare to bring outdoor plants inside.

Winter

Water less frequently, stop fertilizing, provide extra humidity, and keep plants away from cold drafts.
