Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I water my plants?

Watering frequency depends on the plant type, pot size, and environmental conditions. Most indoor plants need watering when the top inch of soil feels dry. Check the specific care instructions for each plant as some prefer to dry out completely between waterings while others need consistently moist soil.

How do I know which plants are suitable for my home?

Consider the light conditions in your home, your experience level, and how much time you can dedicate to plant care. Our website provides detailed information about each plant's light requirements, watering needs, and difficulty level. You can also contact us for personalized recommendations.

How do I repot my plant?

Repotting is best done in spring or early summer. Choose a pot 1-2 inches larger than the current one with drainage holes. Use fresh potting mix appropriate for your plant type. Gently remove the plant from its current pot, loosen the roots slightly, and place it in the new pot at the same depth it was growing before. Water thoroughly after repotting.

What should I do if I see pests on my plants?

Isolate the affected plant immediately to prevent spreading. Identify the pest (common ones include spider mites, mealybugs, and aphids) and treat accordingly. You can use neem oil, insecticidal soap, or other organic treatments. Feel free to contact us for specific advice based on your situation.
